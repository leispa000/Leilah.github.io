About Me

I am a junior at Howard University pursuing a Bachelor of Arts in Sociology and History, originally from Seattle, Washington. As a member of the Humanities and Social Science Scholars Program (H3SP), I engage deeply with interdisciplinary approaches that examine the social forces shaping our world. My academic interests center on race and gender, with a particular focus on how historical systems of inequality continue to influence contemporary social institutions. I am especially interested in the intersections of identity, power, and structural inequality, and how these dynamics manifest in areas such as education, sports, and access to opportunity.

Unequal Streets:
Mapping Crime & Public Safety Disparities
Across Washington, D.C.'s Wards

Using over 24,000 incident-level records from DC's Metropolitan Police Department, this project investigates how crime type, frequency, and severity vary across DC's eight wards in 2025. Through data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and 8 visualizations — including an interactive geographic map of gun-related incidents — the project reveals stark disparities: Wards 7 and 8, east of the Anacostia River, bear a disproportionate share of violent crime, reflecting decades of systemic disinvestment.

AI Innovators:
What Makes a Song Go Viral?
Analyzing Spotify Audio Features & Popularity

Using a dataset of 114,000 Spotify tracks across 114 genres, this project explores what audio features — such as danceability, energy, tempo, and valence — most strongly predict a song's popularity. Through data wrangling, exploratory analysis, and visualization in Python, we uncovered that fewer than 5% of songs ever break a popularity score of 70, raising the question: what truly separates a hit from the noise?
